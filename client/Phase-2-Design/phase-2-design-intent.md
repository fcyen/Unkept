# Phase 2 - design thoughts

# 1. Getting inspiration from websites

### Readymag

- What I like
    - video (moving pictures) and music enhance the atmosphere of the page
- What I don’t like
    - some website are beautiful but hard to navigate, I don’t know where to click, lack of flow in interactions
    - too editorial and abstract

### Exposure.co

- What I like
    - Is a good start to what my UI can look like, standard layout that looks good
- What I don’t like
    - Lack of interactions, and placeholder for notes

### MagnumPhotos

- What I like
    - good balance of photos and text, works well on mobile too

### PolarSteps app

- What I like
    - The UX of viewing stories, familiar mechanism to popular apps like Instagram and Tinder - tap on the right to go to the next photo, slide up to see the text
- What I don’t like
    - Adding past trips takes too many steps, I was expecting that when I enter the Start to End Date, the app will just get the photos in my album and create an initial template of the trip. Instead, I had to add the ‘steps’ myself.

### DayOne

- What I like
    - Simple markdown format of the journal
    - Different views: List, Calendar, Media, Map, all offers great perspective of the past journals

## Emotional target

I want users to have something easily accessible that they can show it to other people and talk about their trips.
